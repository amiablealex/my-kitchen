Based on your pantry list, you have the ingredients to make a really sophisticated fusion blend. Since you have Ras el Hanout (North African) and Pul Biber (Turkish/Aleppo pepper), we can move away from a "standard" Indian curry and make something aromatic, slightly sweet, and fruity-spicy.

I call this blend "The Golden Anise Fusion."

It uses Fennel Seeds to provide a sweet anise punch that cuts through the blended onion base, while the Pul Biber gives a mild, fruity heat that pairs perfectly with spinach.

Here is the paste mix for two people.

The Golden Anise Paste
The Whole Spices (For texture and top notes):

1 tsp Coriander Seeds (Roughly crushed with the back of a spoon or pestle)

1 tsp Fennel Seeds (Keep these whole; they add amazing bursts of sweetness)

The Powders (The body of the curry):

1 tsp Turmeric (For that deep golden color)

1 tsp Cumin

1 tsp Ras el Hanout (This adds complexity with hints of cinnamon and rose)

1 tsp Pul Biber (If you like it hot, add 1/2 tsp Chilli Flakes too, but Pul Biber is all about flavor)

1/2 tsp Ginger (Powder)

1/2 tsp Tandoori Masala (To add a savory depth to the chicken)

The binder:

2 tbsp Olive Oil

1 tbsp Water (just to loosen it into a sludge/paste)

How to cook it with your ingredients
Since you are blending your onions (which creates a restaurant-style "gravy" consistency), you need to be careful not to burn the spices, but they must be cooked to remove the raw floury taste.

Prep the Paste: Mix all the ingredients listed above in a small bowl. It should look like a thick, dark mud.

The "Bloom": In your pan, after you have removed the softened onions to blend them, add a splash more oil. Get it medium-hot.

Fry the Paste: Drop your spice paste into the hot oil. Fry it for about 30–60 seconds. It will smell incredible immediately. Keep it moving so it doesn't burn.

Add the Liquid: Pour your blended onion mixture directly onto the frying spices. It will sizzle loudly. Stir well to combine the oil/spices with the onion gravy.

Simmer: Let the onion gravy and spices simmer together for 5 minutes. This is crucial for the flavors to meld.

The Chicken: Add your fried chicken chunks into the sauce.

The Spinach: Add the spinach right at the end. Since you are using fennel and ras el hanout, spinach is the perfect vegetable here. Let it wilt into the sauce.

Optional Finish: If you find the curry is too thick, add a splash of water. If it tastes a bit bitter, a tiny pinch of sugar helps bring out the fennel.

Cooking everything together on a single large baking tray is the best way to do this. The potatoes and Mediterranean vegetables will cook in the olive oil together, absorbing each other's flavors, while the salmon will be added at the very end so it stays perfectly juicy and doesn't overcook.

Because a 400g salmon fillet only takes about 15 minutes, we will use a staggered timing method.

Here is your step-by-step game plan.

The Prep & Tray Arrangement
Oven Setup: Preheat your oven to 200°C (180°C fan).

The Potatoes: Dice your potatoes into small, uniform cubes (about 1.5cm). Smaller cubes mean they will cook at the exact same rate as the vegetables.

The Arrangement: Grab your largest baking tray.

Spread the potatoes, courgettes, peppers, red onion, and smashed garlic across the tray.

Important: Leave a clear, empty space right in the center (or on one side) of the tray where your salmon fillet will go later.

The Oil Coating: Drizzle everything generously with extra virgin olive oil, salt, pepper, and dried herbs. Toss the vegetables and potatoes around to coat them completely, keeping that designated salmon spot clear.

The Timing Breakdown (45 Minutes Total)
Phase 1: The Veg & Potato Roast (Minutes 0 to 30)
Action: Pop the tray of vegetables and potatoes into the oven.

Timer: Set for 30 minutes.

At the 15-minute mark: Give the potatoes and veg a gentle toss and turn with a spatula so they brown evenly, then return to the oven.

Phase 2: Prep the Salmon (During Phase 1)
While the vegetables are roasting, take your 400g salmon fillet out of the fridge.

Pat the skin and flesh completely dry with a paper towel (this stops it from steaming).

Rub it with a little olive oil, salt, and pepper.

Phase 3: Add the Salmon (Minutes 30 to 45)
Action: When your 30-minute timer goes off, carefully pull the baking tray out of the oven. The potatoes should be softening and the veg looking glossy and slightly charred.

Place the Salmon: Place your 400g salmon fillet (skin-side down, if it has skin) right into that empty space you saved.

Timer: Put the tray back in the oven for 12 to 15 minutes.

How to Tell When It’s Done
At the 45-minute mark, check the tray:

The Salmon: Take a paring knife or fork and gently press the thickest part of the salmon. It should flake easily and look opaque in the center.

The Potatoes & Veg: The potatoes should be tender when pierced with a fork, and the veggies should be beautifully collapsed, jammy, and sitting in a pool of delicious, flavorful olive oil.

To Serve: Transfer the salmon to a serving platter, then spoon the oily, rich potatoes and Mediterranean vegetables all around it. Scrape every last drop of the garlic-and-pepper-infused olive oil from the tray right over the salmon and potatoes. Top with a massive squeeze of fresh lemon juice.


That sounds like an incredible meal. Rich, oily Mediterranean vegetables are the ultimate partner for roast salmon—the sweet, jammy veggies and savory olive oil perfectly complement the richness of the fish.
To get that authentic, silkily textured Mediterranean vibe (think Greek Briam or a Spanish Pisto style), we want vegetables that collapse and absorb olive oil beautifully.
Here is how to select, cook, and serve your ultimate Mediterranean side dish.
The Veg Selection
While you could use all of them, the absolute prime candidates for that meltingly tender, oily texture are:

The Stars: Courgette, red bell pepper, and sweet pointed red peppers. These get incredibly sweet and tender.
The Aromatics: Red onion (for its color and sweetness when roasted) and a generous amount of garlic.
The Benchwarmers: Leave the carrot and brown onion out of this specific dish. Carrots stay a bit too crunchy/earthy for a classic jammy Med bake, and red onion outshines brown onion here for vibrant color.
The Recipe: Slow-Roasted Mediterranean Veg Confit
The secret to a truly "oily" and authentic Mediterranean dish isn't just frying things; it’s letting the vegetables slowly bake in a generous bath of good quality extra virgin olive oil until they are completely tender and caramelized.
Prep the Ingredients

Courgette: Cut into thick rounds or chunks (about 2cm). If they are too thin, they will turn to mush.
Peppers (Both types): Deseed and cut into wide strips or chunky squares.
Red Onion: Cut into thick wedges so they don't burn.
Garlic: Peel 4–6 cloves and leave them whole but lightly smashed with the flat of your knife. They will become sweet and buttery.
The Cooking Method

The Assembly: Preheat your oven to 200°C (180°C fan). Grab a large baking dish or roasting tray. You want the vegetables to be snug but mostly in a single layer.
The Olive Oil Bath: Toss all the prepared vegetables and garlic directly into the tray. Drizzle very generously with extra virgin olive oil—you want enough to thoroughly coat everything and leave a pool at the bottom of the dish (about 4–5 tablespoons).
The Seasoning: Keep it simple and authentic. Season generously with sea salt, cracked black pepper, and a pinch of dried oregano or thyme if you have it. Toss everything with your hands to ensure maximum coverage.
The Roast: Roast for about 35–45 minutes. Halfway through, give them a gentle toss. You are looking for charred edges on the peppers, caramelized onions, and courgettes that have completely softened and absorbed the oil.
How to Serve it with your Salmon
The Golden Rule: Do not leave the oil behind in the baking dish! That oil is now infused with sweet pepper, garlic, and onion flavors. It is pure liquid gold.

Plating: Spoon a generous mound of the glossy, warm vegetables onto the plate alongside (or right underneath) your roast salmon.
The Finish: Drizzle the remaining warm, flavored olive oil from the baking tray right over both the vegetables and the salmon.
The Acid Kick: Cut a fresh lemon into wedges. Squeeze a hit of fresh lemon juice over everything right before eating. The acidity cuts through the richness of the olive oil and the salmon, tying the whole plate together beautifully.
